# AI Usage Policy

HashHive welcomes AI-assisted development. Many of us use AI coding
assistants daily and consider them valuable tools. This policy exists
not to restrict AI usage, but to ensure that every contribution
meets the same standard of understanding and accountability regardless
of how it was produced.

## Rules

- **Every line of code must be read and understood by the human
  responsible for it.** If you cannot explain what your changes do
  and how they interact with the rest of the system without the aid
  of AI tools, do not submit them.

- **AI usage must be disclosed.** State the tool you used (e.g.
  Claude Code, Cursor, Copilot, Amp) and the extent of AI assistance
  in your pull request description.

- **AI-assisted text (issues, discussions, PR descriptions) must be
  reviewed and edited by a human before submission.** AI tends toward
  verbosity and filler. Trim it down. Make sure the content reflects
  your actual understanding, not a language model's approximation of it.

- **No AI-generated media (art, images, videos, audio).** Text and
  code are the only acceptable AI-generated content, per the other
  rules in this policy.

- **Low-effort AI slop will be rejected.** Contributions that are
  clearly unreviewed AI output -- hallucinated APIs, nonsensical
  variable names, generic boilerplate that ignores project conventions --
  will be closed without review. Repeat offenders may be blocked from
  future contributions.

## Why This Policy Exists

AI tools are force multipliers. A developer who understands the
codebase can use AI to move faster without sacrificing quality. A
developer who does not understand the codebase will use AI to produce
plausible-looking code that breaks in subtle ways, and the burden of
finding those breaks falls on maintainers.

This policy protects maintainers. Every pull request is reviewed by
humans. Submitting work you do not understand wastes their time and
yours.

## For New Contributors

If you are learning and want to use AI to help you understand the
codebase, that is great. But submit code that you wrote and understand,
not code that an AI wrote and you hope is correct. We are happy to
help you learn through code review -- that only works if the code
reflects your understanding.
